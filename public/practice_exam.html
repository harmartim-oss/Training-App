Privacy Law

Under PIPEDA, when must an organization notify the Privacy Commissioner of a personal information breach that poses a real risk of significant harm?

Explanation

The correct answer is D) As soon as feasible after discovery. Under PIPEDA, organizations must notify the Privacy Commissioner of breaches that pose a real risk of significant harm "as soon as feasible" rather than within a specific timeframe. This allows organizations to conduct a preliminary assessment while ensuring timely notification.
Reference: PIPEDA Breach of Security Safeguards Regulations, section 3
