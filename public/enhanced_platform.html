3.1 Introduction to Ontario's AI Directive

Ontario's Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence Directive, effective December 1, 2024, establishes six core principles for the ethical and accountable use of AI systems in government operations. This comprehensive framework ensures that AI technologies benefit the people of Ontario while managing associated risks.

Key Learning Objective

By the end of this section, you will understand how to implement the six AI principles in your organization and create effective AI governance structures.

The Six Principles of Responsible AI

1. Beneficial Use

AI must benefit the people of Ontario and consider diverse user needs.

2. Justified & Proportionate

AI use must serve well-defined purposes with reliable, valid systems.

3. Safe & Privacy-Protective

Maintain data privacy and security while minimizing risks.

4. Non-Discriminatory

Respect human rights and ensure fairness across all communities.

5. Transparent

Provide meaningful explanations of AI decisions and processes.

6. Accountable

Maintain clear human oversight and responsibility for AI systems.

Interactive Scenario: Municipal Chatbot Implementation

Your municipality wants to implement an AI chatbot to help residents with service inquiries. Apply the six principles:

Knowledge Check (3-Minute Retrieval Practice)

Which principle requires organizations to provide accessible channels for public inquiries about AI use?

Correct! ✓

The "Transparent" principle specifically requires providing accessible channels for public inquiries about AI use in processes, services, or programs.

1.1 Understanding PIPEDA's Fair Information Principles

The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) governs how private-sector organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information in the course of commercial activities. Understanding its ten Fair Information Principles is crucial for Ontario businesses operating under federal jurisdiction.

Principle 1: Accountability

An organization is responsible for personal information under its control and shall designate an individual or individuals who are accountable for the organization's compliance with the following principles.

Practical Application for Small Businesses: This means your organization must designate a privacy officer or assign privacy responsibilities to a specific role. For small businesses, this might be the owner, manager, or HR director. The key is ensuring someone has clear accountability for privacy compliance.

💡 Implementation Tip

Create a simple organizational chart showing privacy responsibilities and ensure all staff know who to contact with privacy questions or concerns.

Under PIPEDA, which of the following best describes the "Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention" principle?

Correct Answer: A

This principle requires that personal information only be used for the stated purpose unless consent is obtained or law requires disclosure. It also mandates that information be retained only as long as necessary.

The Detect (DE) function focuses on developing and implementing appropriate activities to identify the occurrence of a cybersecurity event. This includes continuous monitoring and detection processes.
